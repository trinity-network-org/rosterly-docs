Captain Onboarding — Rosterly

Welcome, Captain! This guide walks you through everything from your first login to running game day. As a captain you own your team's roster, set your lineups, and keep your players organized for the season.

📷 _Screenshot placeholder: Rosterly home screen for a captain_

1. Getting in — register, then log in

Rosterly is invite-only, and everyone registers first. You'll receive a personal captain code from your league admin (tied to your phone number).

First time — register:

  1. Open the Rosterly app and tap Create an account.
  2. Enter your phone number, your captain code, and your name.
  3. Tap Create Account — you're registered as a Captain.

Every time after — log in:

  • On the sign-in screen, enter your phone number + your captain code.
📷 _Screenshot placeholder: Register screen, then Sign-in screen_
Note: You can't log in until you've registered. The code must match your role — a code for a different role won't log you in. If it says your number isn't registered, register first (and check the number matches what your admin set).

2. Join your league

Being a captain doesn't automatically put you in a league — you request access, and your admin approves it.

  1. Open the Rosters tab (or the menu ☰).
  2. You'll see a list of available leagues.
  3. Tap Request Access on your league.
  4. Wait for your admin to approve — you'll get a notification when you're in.
📷 _Screenshot placeholder: league list with "Request Access" button_

3. Build your roster

Once you're approved, build out your team.

  1. Go to the Rosters tab.
  2. Tap ＋ / Build Roster to add players.
  3. For each player set their:
  • Name
  • Positions they can play
  • Expertise (novice / middle / expert)
  • Gender (used to balance the lineup)
  1. Edit or remove players any time.
📷 _Screenshot placeholder: roster builder with a player being added_

Mark your co-captains (optional)

If you want help managing lineups, tap a player on your roster and toggle Co-Capt. Co-captains can join the Huddle with you to build lineups together (see step 6).

📷 _Screenshot placeholder: roster row with the Co-Capt toggle_

4. See your schedule — My Games

Open My Games (Home card or menu ☰) to see your team's full league schedule: upcoming matchups, past results, and each game's lineup.

Each game card shows how many of your players have marked themselves In, so you know who you're working with before you set a lineup.

📷 _Screenshot placeholder: My Games list with availability counts_

5. Set your lineup

For each game you submit a lineup — the batting order and fielding assignments. You have two ways to do it:

Option A — Submit it yourself

  1. Open the game in My Games (or Play Game).
  2. Build the batting order and fielding positions from your roster.
  3. Tap Submit Lineup.

You can re-submit to make changes right up until the game is finished.

📷 _Screenshot placeholder: lineup builder_

Option B — Build it together in a Huddle

If you have co-captains, use a Huddle — a shared room where you all agree on the lineup before game day.

  1. Open Huddles from the menu ☰.
  2. Open the game's room — it has Chat, Batting, and Fielding tabs.
  3. Everyone edits the draft together (changes sync live).
  4. Co-captains can drop 👍 / 👎 with notes to weigh in.
  5. When ready, you finalize it (or hand the "gavel" to a co-captain to finalize). Finalizing locks the room and saves it as your official lineup.
📷 _Screenshot placeholder: Huddle room (Batting tab)_
Note: Huddles may be turned off for your league. If you don't see the Huddles menu item, your admin hasn't enabled it — just use Option A.

6. Game day

On game day the referee runs the match: they open the game, use your submitted lineup (or type one in), score it live, and submit the final result.

Your job before then: make sure your lineup is submitted and your roster is up to date. After the game finishes, the score and your players' stats update automatically.

📷 _Screenshot placeholder: a finished game showing the final score_

7. Track the season — Standings

Open Standings (Home card or menu ☰) to follow league standings, scores, and results as games finish.
